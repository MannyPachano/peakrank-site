Local SEO strategy

How to do local keyword research for a service-area business

The goal is not raw search volume. It is matching how real customers phrase problems and neighborhoods, then building pages that intercept that intent. Here is an end-to-end process you can run with mostly free tools.

By PeakRanking · April 2026 · ~2,400 words · 11 min read Keyword research

Why most local keyword research goes wrong

Picture a cleaning company in Phoenix (composite example). They target house cleaning because the volume looks attractive and the topic fits.

The issue: that phrase is national and informational as often as it is local. Many results are franchises, marketplaces, or editorial content with years of authority. A single-city operator usually cannot win that head term.

Meanwhile house cleaning service Phoenix, deep cleaning Phoenix AZ, and move-out cleaning Scottsdale, the phrases people use right before booking, may have no dedicated pages at all.

The common mistake: chasing generic, high-volume phrases instead of specific, geo-modified queries customers actually use. For service-area businesses, the geographic layer is what keeps competition realistic and intent high. The anatomy of a local keyword

High-converting local keywords usually combine a service, a location, and often an intent modifier. Seeing the pattern helps you brainstorm systematically.

The local keyword formula

Service+Location+Intent modifier =High-intent local query

High intent

emergency plumber + Denver CO + 24 hour → 24 hour emergency plumber Denver CO

High intent

roof repair + Charlotte + storm → storm-damage roof repair queries

Medium

HVAC service + Nashville TN → core trade + city

Medium

lawn care + Austin + residential → niche + geo

Research

how much does + electrician + cost + Seattle → pricing and education content

Modifiers like emergency, 24 hour, near me, same day, licensed, or free estimate signal urgency and budget. Someone searching emergency plumber Denver is closer to a dial than someone reading generic plumbing tips.

Understanding four types of local search intent

Map intent before you map URLs. Your site should cover all four; each needs a different page type.

Urgency

emergency AC repair near me burst pipe plumber open now 24 hour locksmith Houston

Fast pages, visible phone, mobile-first layout. These users call quickly; friction costs the job.

Ready to hire

HVAC contractor Memphis TN house painting service Portland landscaping company near me

Primary money keywords. Use service-plus-location pages with proof, coverage, and reviews.

Research

how much does gutter cleaning cost best time to replace a roof signs you need a new water heater

Blog and guide content. Captures people earlier; links forward into service pages.

Comparing

best pest control company Dallas top rated electricians Chicago reviews licensed vs unlicensed contractor

GBP, reviews, and reputation weight heavily. Content supports trust; listings close the loop.

Service-area businesses: your specific keyword challenge

If you travel to customers, you serve many cities but cannot be equally strong everywhere on day one. Prioritize geography instead of spraying thin pages across every zip.

SAB keyword priority framework

Group keywords by location tier, then expand outward as pages earn traction.

  • Primary city: Full service-page set first (one core service, one primary URL focus).
  • High-value neighbors: Three to five cities with real revenue or growth goals; dedicated location pages with unique detail.
  • Secondary footprint: Expand after core cities work; avoid duplicate templates with only the city swapped.
  • Neighborhoods and suburbs: In crowded metros, hyper-local pages can win where a generic city page stalls.

Step-by-step: how to build your local keyword list

1

List every specific service

Not “plumbing,” but drain cleaning, leak detection, water heater install, and so on. Each line item is its own keyword family and often its own page.

Cleaning company: deep clean, move-out, recurring residential, carpet, post-construction, office, short-term rental turnover.

2

Build your location modifier list

Cities, towns, and neighborhoods you actually serve or want. Include common variants (Phoenix, Phoenix AZ, Phoenix Arizona).

Phoenix metro: Scottsdale, Tempe, Chandler, Gilbert, Mesa, Glendale, Peoria, Paradise Valley.

3

Mine autocomplete and People Also Ask

Type service + city in Google and note suggestions. PAA boxes surface question patterns worth answering on page.

plumber Phoenix may surface 24-hour, emergency, estimate, and same-day variants.

4

Validate volume with Keyword Planner

Use a free Google Ads account, set geography to your market, and compare combinations. Low volume with sharp intent routinely beats broad national numbers you cannot rank for.

Commercial kitchen cleaning with modest volume can still clear high ticket sizes per job.

5

Review competitors who already rank

Search your head money term, open top organic results, and note their URL structure and headings. Lightweight tools can list terms a domain ranks for; those are demand signals.

6

Pull queries from Search Console

In Search Console, open Performance, then Search results. You will see real queries, clicks, and average position. Positions roughly 4 to 20 are often the fastest wins with on-page tuning.

The right tools: free and paid

Google Keyword Planner Free

Volume and competition estimates per keyword. Tie it to a tight geographic target, not nationwide defaults.

Tip: set location to your service city or region.

Google Search Console Free

Shows queries you already earn impressions for, plus CTR and position. Best ground truth for an existing site.

Tip: sort by position 11 to 30 for near-page-one opportunities.

Autocomplete and PAA Free

Direct signals from Google’s UI. Use private or incognito browsing to reduce personalization noise.

Tip: repeat for each priority city modifier.

Semrush, Ahrefs, or similar Paid

Competitive gap analysis, difficulty scores, and bulk exports. Useful once free sources are exhausted or markets are crowded.

Tip: pricing tiers change; trial paid tools before committing.

Mapping keywords to pages: the one rule that matters

One primary commercial intent per URL. Do not load two unrelated money phrases onto one page and expect both to stick. Support the primary term with closely related variants in headings and body copy.

Page type Primary keyword Supporting keywords Intent
Homepage plumber Austin TX plumbing service Austin, Austin plumber Transactional
Service page drain cleaning Austin clogged drain Austin, drain service Austin Transactional
Service page water heater installation Austin tankless water heater Austin, replacement Austin Transactional
Location page plumber Round Rock TX plumbing Round Rock, Round Rock emergency plumber Transactional
Location page plumber Cedar Park TX plumbing Cedar Park, Cedar Park TX plumber Urgency
Blog post how much does drain cleaning cost in Austin drain cleaning price, plumber cost Austin Research
Blog post signs you need to replace your water heater water heater lifespan, failure signs Research

Link research content to the matching service URL. That passes relevance and helps users move from education to booking in one clear path. Strong entity signals also reinforce NAP and citation consistency across the web.

A note on zero and low-volume keywords

Keyword tools underestimate hyper-local demand. A line that shows ten monthly searches may still be worth a page if each conversion is valuable.

Low-volume insight

Prefer sharp commercial intent inside your service geography over vague national volume. A precise B2B maintenance phrase with modest reported volume can fund the work if closes are large enough.

Putting it into practice: your first week

Day 1: List eight to twelve discrete services.

Day 2: List every city or neighborhood you would drive to.

Day 3: Run service-plus-primary-city pairs through Keyword Planner, then capture autocomplete and PAA ideas in a sheet.

Day 4: Review Search Console for queries in positions roughly 4 to 20.

Day 5: Assign one primary keyword per page; note gaps that need new URLs or rewrites.

Takeaway

Local keyword research for SABs is about high-intent language inside your real footprint, not leaderboard volume. Build one service and one city at a time, link research to money pages, and repeat. That compounds while competitors chase generic terms they will not win.
